# Work Page Illustration Prompts

Optimized for Google Gemini / NanoBanana. All three use the same style and constraints, only the subject changes.

---

## 1. Iceberg (Product)

```
Create a sumi-e Japanese ink wash painting of an iceberg.
Style: traditional East Asian ink wash (sumi-e), monochromatic using only black, dark gray, and light gray ink tones on pure white background.
Composition: vertical orientation, the iceberg tip is small and sits in the upper third, rendered with dark bold confident brushstrokes showing sharp edges and texture. Below a thin suggested waterline the submerged mass is massive, at least 4x larger than the tip, rendered with progressively lighter gray ink washes that dissolve and fade at the edges.
Texture: visible brushstroke texture on the tip, wet ink wash bleeds and diffusion on the submerged part, scattered ink splatters around the waterline area.
The waterline should be subtle, just a faint horizontal ink wash, not a hard line.
IMPORTANT: the entire illustration must be fully contained within the canvas. Leave generous white margin space on all four sides (top, bottom, left, right). No ink, no brushstrokes, no splatters, no shapes touching or bleeding past the edges of the canvas.
No text, no labels, no annotations.
White background, no border, no frame.
```

## 2. Mountain (GTM)

```
Create a sumi-e Japanese ink wash painting of a single mountain with two distinct paths leading to the summit.
Style: traditional East Asian ink wash (sumi-e), monochromatic using only black, dark gray, and light gray ink tones on pure white background.
Composition: vertical orientation, the mountain is centered with its base above the lower edge of the canvas, not touching the bottom. The summit is sharp, rendered in dark bold ink with visible brushstroke texture. The mountain body has two visible ridges or paths: one steep and direct on the left side, one longer and gradual winding on the right side. The paths are suggested through lighter ink wash tones and subtle line work, not drawn as literal trails.
The mountain base dissolves into soft gray ink wash, fading into the white background. Layered ridges at varying opacities create depth, darker in front, lighter behind.
Texture: bold dry brush strokes at the summit, wet ink wash bleeds on the lower slopes, small ink splatters near the peak.
No trees, no clouds, no sun, no additional elements. Just the mountain and its paths.
IMPORTANT: the entire illustration must be fully contained within the canvas. Leave generous white margin space on all four sides (top, bottom, left, right). No ink, no brushstrokes, no splatters, no shapes touching or bleeding past the edges of the canvas.
No text, no labels, no annotations.
White background, no border, no frame.
```

## 3. Moon, Water and Fog (Marketing)

```
Create a sumi-e Japanese ink wash painting of a full moon reflected in still water with fog on the surface.
Style: traditional East Asian ink wash (sumi-e), monochromatic using only black, dark gray, and light gray ink tones on pure white background.
Composition: vertical orientation. The moon sits in the upper third as a soft pale gray circular wash, not a hard outlined circle but a gentle ink wash circle with slightly uneven edges. Below the center is a still water surface rendered as very light horizontal ink wash strokes. The moon's reflection appears in the water, slightly elongated and broken by gentle ripples suggested through subtle ink wash variations. Between the moon and the water, fog is rendered as diffused soft gray ink that fades horizontally and dissipates well before reaching the side edges of the canvas.
The overall composition should be the lightest and most atmospheric of the three: minimal ink, maximum white space. The fog should feel like breath on the paper.
Texture: the moon is a single soft wash, the water is thin horizontal ink strokes with varying opacity, the fog is wet ink fading outward with no defined edges.
No mountains, no trees, no boats, no additional elements. Just moon, water, fog.
IMPORTANT: the entire illustration must be fully contained within the canvas. Leave generous white margin space on all four sides (top, bottom, left, right). No ink, no brushstrokes, no splatters, no shapes touching or bleeding past the edges of the canvas. The fog and water strokes must end well before the left and right edges.
No text, no labels, no annotations.
White background, no border, no frame.
```

## Usage Notes

- Export as SVG or AVIF with white background
- White background disappears on the site via `mix-blend-multiply` CSS
- All illustrations use vertical orientation to fit tall cards
- Monochromatic only — accent color (#ff4309) is added via the site, not the illustrations
