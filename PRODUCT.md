## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience of potential clients (founders, PMs, execs evaluating Jan for strategy or product work), recruiters and employers (looking to bring Jan in-house), and professional peers (builders, designers, strategists wanting to connect). They arrive with curiosity or intent — either evaluating Jan's thinking or deciding if this is someone worth reaching out to. The site must make a fast, authentic impression and leave a distinctive memory.

### Brand Personality
**Voice**: Honest, direct, no filler words. Says what others don't bother saying.
**Tone**: Warm but sharp. Thoughtful without being precious.
**3 words + 1**: Curious · Confident · Energetic · No Bullshit

The interface should feel like Jan himself — someone who thinks clearly, moves with purpose, and doesn't hide behind corporate formality or trendy aesthetics.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Light mode only. Clean off-white ground (neutral-100), strong neutral-800 text, signature orange-red accent (#ff4309 / `--color-accent`).
**Font**: Gilroy — custom, personality-forward, not a generic system font.
**Feel**: Editorial and considered. Intentional whitespace. Asymmetric layouts. Text-forward without feeling like a wall of copy.

**Anti-references** — this site must NOT look like:
- Generic agency template (card grids, hero + stock photo, hollow "we help brands" copy)
- Flashy dev portfolio (dark mode, neon glows, code-as-decoration, Three.js hero)
- Corporate consultant (navy + grey, formal, stiff)
- AI slop (glassmorphism everywhere, gradient text headings, cyan-on-dark, bouncy animations)
- Anything that feels impersonal or inauthentic

**References**: None given explicitly — the existing site already has the right instincts (geometric SVG logo, strikethrough link interactions, liquid glass nav, bold fluid typography). Build on this, don't abandon it.

### Design Principles
1. **Authentic over polished** — Personality beats perfection. If it feels like it could be anyone's site, it's wrong.
2. **Words do the work** — Copy and typography carry the message. Decoration is a bonus, never a crutch.
3. **Bold without noise** — The accent (#ff4309) should feel like a punchline, not wallpaper. Use it with purpose.
4. **Motion earns its place** — Every animation must have a reason. State change, emphasis, delight — never just "because it looks cool."
5. **Accessibility as respect** — Target WCAG AA as a baseline. Not as checkbox compliance, but because users deserve it.
